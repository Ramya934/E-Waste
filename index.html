About Our E-Waste Hub

An e-waste hub typically refers to a centralized facility or a designated area where electronic waste (e-waste) is collected, sorted, processed, and often recycled or disposed of in an environmentally friendly manner. E-waste includes discarded electronic devices such as computers, mobile phones, televisions, refrigerators, and other electronic appliances.
E-waste hubs play a crucial role in managing the disposal of electronic waste, which can contain hazardous materials such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants. Improper disposal of e-waste can lead to environmental pollution and pose health risks to humans and wildlife.
